Future of the Industry

Where to next for this ever changing and ever growing powerhouse of an industry?

The Future is Now!

Game delivery has already begun to change yet again, this time to a subscription based model. The tech giants like Google and Microsoft are already developing ways for games to be streamed to anywhere in the world with an internet connection so that games need only be stored in a central server.

This avoids the need to own a dedicated, bulky console and only requires a smart TV at the very worst, as all the computations are done on the server, not locally.

Game Formats

Virtual Reality (or VR) has started to take off, it invloves tracking your head and hands to allow interaction with the game's environment. This technology is quite resource intensive and thus is not as accessible as other forms of entertainment, but as the tech improves, so does the price to performance.

Racing and flying simulators are also getting a boost as more accurate controllers and game engines (heh) are developed and released. These allow the game to be more accurate and give a more realistic feel. These are some of just a few ways that game devs are dreaming up to expand games to a wider audience.

Ingame Intelligence

As games progress, so too, do the non-player characters, or NPCs that dwell in those games. NPCs were coded on an if/then style, but these days they have a behaviour tree to allow complex decisons to be made.
