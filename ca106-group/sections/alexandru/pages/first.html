First Game Inventor

There are so many games in todays age that are so complex and intricate that we can't even comprehend how older generations had fun with tiny pixels that would only bounce back and forth. It wasnt even possible for these generations to have access to a fraction of the hardware we have today. Most scientists had the posssiblity to create and play games as their labs had computers that had the capabilities of doing so such as Physicist William Higinbotham. Who created the first ever game. Some say he was the inspiration that lead to the creation of Pong.

It was a tennis game. The reason for its invention was to showcase something new and interesting in the Brookhaven National Laboratory. The exhibitions were rather boring to William Higinbotham so he sought out to create a game using a small analogue computer which could output curves and bouncing balls. To which he called his game “Tennis for Two”, he didn’t realise that his work was such a big hit at the time and didn’t patent his idea. There were always long lines from the visitors waiting to play his game.

It is rather interesting how this scientist is known for just his invention of a videogame rather than his expertise in nuclear arms control. Where he helped found the Federation of American Scientists, while also serving as its chairman and executive secretary.
