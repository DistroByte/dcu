Role-Playing Games

What is a Role-Playing Game?

Known to gamers as RPG, a Role-Playing Game is a gaming genre that revolves around the player to take on a character from a storyline and advance through that storyline. As the game progresses, the character will have to become stronger in order to deal with more challenging foes.

Attributes of Role-Playing Games

As mentioned in the previous heading, RPGs have a storyline that the player will follow in order to complete the game. This storyline involves overcoming certain obstacles in order to progress. This usually involves the strengthening of the character's physical and mental attributes. In some RPGs, you'll either have more attributes that are already listed or none at all. We'll explore more of these in the examples section

Earliest RPG

Dragonstomper on the Atari - 1982

Examples of RPGs

Genshin Impact: This RPG allows the player to take on the role of 4 characters that can change from one another Each character has a unique set of skills and abilities that make them different from one another. The objective of the game is to defeat certain bosses and foes to be able to find your sibling.

Super Mario Bros Franchise: Each Super Mario Bros Franchise game has their own quirks that set them apart from the others but the goal of the game is always the same. As the character Mario, you must go through different worlds and castles defeating bossess until you unlock the final world, where you have a chance to defeat the final boss and save Princess Peach.

MMORPG

This is a popular genre that fits under RPGs. MMORPG stands for Massively Multiplayer Online Role-Playing Game. The difference between this genre and it's parent genre is that include the presence of other players.

Examples of MMORPGs

Runescape: Runescape is an MMORPG set in the fantasy world of Gielinor. This world is divided into different kingdoms, regions and cities. Players can travel throughout the world, battling mythical beasts.
